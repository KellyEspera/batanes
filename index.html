Discover the Soul of Batanes Cuisine

Welcome to the Batanes Cuisine Explorer! Our journey began with a deep admiration for the unique culinary heritage of the Ivatan people, the resilient inhabitants of the Philippines' northernmost islands. Batanes is a land of breathtaking landscapes, windswept hills, and a culture deeply connected to the rhythms of nature. This connection is beautifully reflected in its food.

Ivatan cuisine is a testament to resourcefulness and respect for the environment. Shaped by the islands' unique geography and the challenges of its remote location, the food is traditionally simple, hearty, and profoundly local. It relies heavily on what the land and sea provide: an abundance of root crops like Wakay (sweet potato), Uvi (yam), and Sudi (taro); fresh catch from the surrounding waters, especially the iconic Dibang (flying fish) and Payi (lobster); and ingenious methods of food preservation.

Dishes like Luñis (pork slow-cooked and preserved in its own lard), Vunes (dried taro stalks), and Uvud Balls (made from banana pith) tell stories of Ivatan ingenuity and adaptation. The vibrant yellow of Supas (turmeric rice) brightens every meal, while ingredients like native garlic and local onions add distinct flavors that are unmistakably Batanes. Sustainability isn't a trend here; it's a way of life, passed down through generations.

This website is our humble attempt to share the authentic flavors and culinary traditions of Batanes with you. Explore our collection of recipes, learn through tutorials, and discover where to find these unique local ingredients. We hope to offer you a glimpse into the resilient, vibrant culture of the Ivatans and their deep, respectful relationship with their bountiful islands.

Join us in celebrating the simple, honest, and delicious food of Batanes!
